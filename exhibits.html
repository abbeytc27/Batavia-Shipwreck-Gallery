Consignments

The Batavia voyage was the maiden voyage of the Dutch East India Company (VOC) ship, the Batavia, which sailed from Texel, Netherlands, on October 27, 1628.

Ins and Outs

The Batavia shipwreck occurred on June 4, 1629, when the Dutch East India Company (VOC) ship, the Batavia, hit a reef near the Houtman Abrolhos islands off the coast of Western Australia.

The History

After the events of the Batavia shipwreck, the remaining survivors were eventually rescued and taken to Batavia (modern-day Jakarta).

The Boat

The ship was captained by Francisco Pelsaert and was carrying a cargo of silver, gold, and valuable trade goods, as well as 341 passengers, including soldiers and colonists destined for the Dutch East Indies.

The Survivors

The ship was on its maiden voyage, carrying a cargo of silver, gold, and valuable trade goods, as well as 341 passengers, including soldiers and colonists destined for the Dutch East Indies.

The Wreck

The wreck site remained largely untouched for centuries until it was rediscovered in the 1960s by a group of divers.
